    Inspirations

    DROD

    Solidus was, first and foremost, inspired by DROD (Deadly Rooms Of Death).

    DROD captivated me as a child, and then again during covid when I discovered GatEB. I was enthralled by the tactical, positional combat which was unlike anything I'd seen anywhere else. My mind wandered to what else the Q/W/Numpad control scheme could achieve, and the seeds of Solidus were planted.

    If you enjoy the chess-like, puzzle elements of Solidus you should absolutely give DROD a try.

    Dwarf Fortress

    Dwarf Fortress gave me a pie-in-the-sky vision of what a team of one or two passionate people can achieve on a project like this.

    The boundlessly expanding complexity of DF's systems, its level of immersion despite limited graphics, and the dedication of its creators to keep the game free-to-play have all been big inspirations. It's continued to be a reminder that a game doesn't have to be polished and accessible for it be to beloved.

    Give Dwarf Fortress a try if you want to push the bounds of what it means to "play" a game.
